About Me

I am a software engineer and an educator who enjoys solving problems. After graduating from Smith College in Northampton, MA, I spent five years in a classroom teaching underprivileged students in order to be part of urban education reform. From my teaching experience, I learned to be eager and perservere to solve problems, find the best solution in a creative way, and always stay motivated.
As education technology evolved and was implemented in classrooms, I became interested in coding, which I found very logical and fun to work on. After taking several online courses, I decided to join General Assembly Software Engineer Immersive Program to learn cutting-edge frontend and backend technologies. Throughout the program, I learned various programming languages and about tech industry. I am hoping to use my skills as a software engineer to continue to solve problems in our society!
